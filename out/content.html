What I'm Into
7 items · books, papers, podcasts, channels
📘
Book
Probabilistic Robotics
Thrun, Burgard & Fox
The canonical SLAM textbook. Dense but worth every page — the Kalman filter and particle filter chapters alone changed how I think about state estimation.
📕
Book
Unreasonable Hospitality
Will Guidara
A friend pointed me to this one, and the premise pulled me in immediately — what does it actually look like to go above and beyond for the people you care about? Excited to explore that question through Guidara's lens.
📄
Paper
Attention Is All You Need
Vaswani et al. (2017)
Read this when exploring sequence modeling for robot trajectory prediction. The self-attention mechanism is elegant — and more applicable to robotics than I expected.
📄
Paper
NeRF: Representing Scenes as Neural Radiance Fields
Mildenhall et al. (2020)
Fascinating approach to scene representation — currently exploring how NeRF-based maps could replace traditional voxel grids in robot navigation.
🎙️
Podcast
Sadhguru Podcast
Sadhguru
I practice meditation, and Sadhguru has a way of articulating things that actually land. His insights have a quiet staying power — the kind that surface at the right moments throughout the day.
▶️
YouTube
Marques Brownlee
MKBHD
From cars to consumer electronics, MKBHD brings a considered perspective to what's happening in tech. Less about specs, more about what products actually mean — and that distinction keeps me coming back.
🎵
Music
Daniel Caesar
Daniel Caesar
The soundtrack to focused work. His music has an effortless warmth that makes long sessions feel lighter — the kind of album you put on and forget to skip a single track.
